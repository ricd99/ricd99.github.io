Usage Notes

  • The divider between the "Active Tasks" and "Completed Tasks" section is draggable; you can dynamically resize the two sections.
  • To create a task, click on the empty space in the Active Tasks section.
  • Swiping is supported. Swipe right on Active Tasks to mark the complete, and swipe right on Completed Tasks to delete them.
  • Dynamic sorting of tasks is supported. "Priority" sorts from High to Low prioirty, and "Due Date" sorts from soonest due (or overdue!) to latest due

My thoughts

  • I wanted to try out a low-code platform because I believed the growth of AI meant low-code technologies would become more relevant. I don't know how true that is, so if you have any insights on this please do tell me.
  • In terms of using Outsystems as a entry point to "real coding", that did not work. When you use low-code technologies, a lot of the work is done behind the scenes. This is no problem (and probably very convienient) for expereinced developers, but for people like me who are trying to learn, this is counterproductive.
  • I often found myself blindly following online tutorials when building this app. That's not going to help me learn.
  • Lesson learned: start with "real coding": python, javascript, learn DSA, etc. We may be living through a time of rapid change, but that doesn't change the fact that knowing the fundamentals is essential.
